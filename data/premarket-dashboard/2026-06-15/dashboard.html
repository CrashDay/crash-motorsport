How To Read Candidates

▾
Evidence
The core reason the stock showed up today. Think of this as the headline case for why it deserves attention.
Why it ranks
The strongest positive factors behind the score, such as earnings, price reaction, repeated source support, or a strong theme.
Risk
The main reasons the idea could fail or become less attractive, even if the story sounds compelling.
Before buying
The confirmation we still want before acting. This is the 'slow down and verify' line.
Trigger / Stop / Target / Sizing
These come from the swing setup overlay and turn a ranked idea into a possible trade plan with entry, risk, upside, and position-size guidance.

Candidate Tags

▾
Buy Candidate
One of the stronger names from today's source mix. It still needs confirmation, but it is a higher-priority idea.
Setup Watch
Interesting, but not quite ready. Usually the story is there, but the chart or confirmation still needs work.
Risk Watch
Worth monitoring mostly for caution. The dashboard sees a live issue or damaged setup that makes the long case weaker.
Score
A ranking signal, not a promise. Higher scores mean the dashboard sees more evidence and better alignment, but the trade still has to earn its entry.

How To Read The Scanner

▾
Trend
The broader chart direction. Uptrend names are usually easier swing longs than mixed or damaged charts.
Vol
Volume compared with the stock's recent average. Higher volume can help confirm that a move has real participation behind it.
RS
Relative strength against SPY and QQQ over recent windows. This helps show whether the stock is outperforming the market or just moving with it.
Sector
Relative strength versus a sector proxy ETF. This helps us avoid names that look fine on their own but are weak versus their closest peer group.
Trigger
What price behavior would make the setup actionable instead of just interesting.
Invalidation
The level or behavior that would tell us the setup has failed and should not be trusted.
Target
The first logical area where price could run into resistance or where partial profits might make sense.
Scores
B is breakout score, P is pullback score, and RS is relative strength score. Higher is generally better, but the setup still needs context and confirmation.
Trade Quality
Sector is sector-relative strength, Fresh measures whether the setup still looks timely instead of stretched, and Event is the amount of earnings or headline risk still hanging over the chart.

Scanner Tags

▾
Setup
The scanner's best read on the kind of trade this chart currently resembles, such as breakout candidate, pullback watch, or risk / avoid.
Pattern
The most notable recent candlestick or price-action signal the scan found. This is a clue, not a trade by itself.
Relative Strength Label
How the stock has been behaving versus SPY and QQQ. Leader means it is outperforming, while lagging or laggard means it is falling behind.
Sector Tag
A quick read on whether the stock is beating or lagging the ETF that best matches its lane, like XLK for software or SMH for semis.

Swing Trade Setup Board

Show strategy playbook

Trend Pullback

Strong ticker or theme that pauses into support instead of chasing a stretched open.

  • Higher low
  • 20-day or prior breakout support holds
  • volume dries up on the pullback

Breakout

Price clears a well-tested resistance level or flag with expanding volume.

  • Close above resistance
  • above-average volume
  • old resistance becomes support

Catalyst Continuation

Fresh earnings, guidance, deal, recommendation, or sector catalyst with broad tape support.

  • holds opening range
  • relative strength versus SPY/QQQ
  • no near-term event trap

Reversal Bounce

Oversold or beaten-down name only after buyers defend a clear level.

  • failed breakdown
  • RSI or momentum turns up
  • tight stop below the reversal low

How To Read This Board

▾
Setup
The short version of what kind of opportunity this is and what we think price is trying to do.
Trigger
What has to happen before the trade is valid. Treat this as the line between watching and acting.
Stop / Invalidation
The price behavior that tells us the idea is wrong. If this breaks, the setup has failed.
First Target Logic
Where the first reasonable profit-taking area is likely to be, based on nearby resistance or the size of the move.
Sizing Caution
Why you might want to use smaller size even if the setup works, usually because of volatility, gaps, or crowded sentiment.
Pass If
The condition that says do not take the trade at all, even if the story sounds good.

Setup Types

▾
Breakout / Momentum
Price is strong and the idea is to buy only if that strength keeps holding, not if the move immediately fades.
Catalyst Continuation
A fresh event like earnings or guidance changed the story, and we are watching for follow-through over the next few sessions.
Reversal Bounce
The stock was weak, but there may be a trade if buyers clearly defend support and the bounce proves itself.
Risk Watch
There is a headline or price-damage problem. This is a caution label, not a green light.
